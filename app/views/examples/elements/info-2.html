2. Re-Importing a Vehicle

When re-importing a used vehicle into Britain you must apply to re-register the vehicle with the DVLA.

Pay VAT, duty and vehicle tax

Use the Notification of Vehicle Arrivals (NOVA) service to tell HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) that you’re importing a vehicle:

  • from within the EU
  • from outside the EU
You usually need to do this within 14 days of arrival.

HMRC will tell you if you have to pay any VAT, duty or tax.

You usually pay VAT and duty through customs when you import a vehicle from outside the EU.

If you’re not a VAT-registered business you can only register your vehicle once you’ve paid your VAT, duty or tax.

You will need:

You should obtain a V55/5 application form to register the vehicle and supporting documents – you can request this here https://www.gov.uk/dvlaforms

  • V55/5
  • ID (Photocopies are acceptable)
    • Name: Driving licence, Passport, Marriage Certificate, Birth Certificate
    • Address: utility bill (within the last 3 months) - eg gas, electricity etc, bank or building society statement (within the last 3 months), medical card, council tax bill for current year
  • Foreign registration document or foreign export certificate* (If these documents are not available; please include a cover letter explaining why. If available; please also include copies of purchase papers/invoices)
  • MOT (if applicable)
  • Insurance Certificate for vehicles being registered to a NI Address (Downloaded or faxed copies are acceptable)
  • Payment for vehicle tax (if not making a SORN declaration)
  • V890 Statutory Off Road Notification declaration form must be submitted with the application if declaring the vehicle off road

Note: No registration Fee is required when re-importing a vehicle as this would have been paid when the vehicle was originally registered
