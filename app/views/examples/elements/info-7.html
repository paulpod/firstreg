7. Registering a Used Imported Vehicle

You will need:

You should obtain a ‘used vehicle import’ pack which includes the relevant V55 application form to register the vehicle and supporting documents – you can request this here https://www.gov.uk/dvlaforms

  • Notification of Vehicle Arrival (NOVA) – see below for more info
  • Type Approval (unless exempt) – see below for more info.
  • Foreign logbook or certificate of export from country of origin or dating evidence from the manufacturer
  • V55/5
  • ID (Photocopies are acceptable)
    • Name: Driving licence, Passport, Marriage Certificate, Birth Certificate
    • Address: utility bill (within the last 3 months) - eg gas, electricity etc, bank or building society statement (within the last 3 months), medical card, council tax bill for current year
  • £55 Registration Fee (if applicable) - See exemptions
  • Fee for Vehicle Tax (if applicable) - See vehicle tax rates
  • MOT (if applicable)
  • Insurance Certificate for vehicles being registered to an NI Address (Downloaded or faxed copies are acceptable)

Pay VAT, duty and vehicle tax

Use the Notification of Vehicle Arrivals (NOVA) service to tell HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) that you’re importing a vehicle:

  • from within the EU
  • from outside the EU
You usually need to do this within 14 days of arrival.

HMRC will tell you if you have to pay any VAT, duty or tax. You usually pay VAT and duty through customs when you import a vehicle from outside the EU.

If you’re not a VAT-registered business you can only register your vehicle once you’ve paid your VAT, duty or tax.

Vehicle Approval

You must show your vehicle meets environmental and safety regulations or has an exemption. You’ll need:

  • a European Certificate of Conformity if your vehicle is right hand drive and has imperial units (mph) - you can get this from the manufacturer
  • a European Certificate of Conformity and a certificate of Mutual Recognition if your vehicle is left hand drive or has metric units (kmph)
  • An Individual Vehicle Approval (IVA) certificate if your vehicle has no European approval.
for Motorbike type approval
  • A European Certificate of Conformity if your motorbike is designed for left hand traffic and has imperial units (mph) on the speedometer - you can get this from the manufacturer
  • a European Certificate of Conformity and a certificate of Mutual Recognition if your motorbike is designed for right hand traffic or has metric units (kmph) on the speedometer
  • Motorcycle Single Vehicle Approval (MSVA) certificate if your vehicle has no European approval.

Supporting documents needed for some vehicles

You may have to send extra forms and documents if your vehicle is:

  • kit-built, rebuilt, kit-converted or radically altered
  • an old vehicle
  • a reconstructed classic vehicle

After you’ve applied

DVLA might need to inspect your vehicle. If your application is approved, DVLA will send you a V5C registration certificate (sometimes called a log book).

This shows:

  • the vehicle’s registration number
  • the vehicle keeper’s name and address
  • other information about the vehicle (the make, vehicle identification number (VIN) and number of previous keepers)

DVLA will also send you the vehicle tax disc and your identity documents. You’ll need to provide a prepaid self-addressed, special delivery envelope if you want the documents returned by special delivery.

DVLA can’t guarantee the return of the documents by a specific date but you should get a new registration certificate within 6 weeks of getting your application.

If you don’t get a response

Contact DVLA if you’ve not had a response after 6 weeks. Don’t wait longer than 3 months or DVLA won’t be able to investigate properly.
